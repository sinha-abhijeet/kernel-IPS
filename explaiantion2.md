# Radial-Kernel-IPS: Full Technical Walkthrough (v2 + Beta Appendix)

This version includes everything from the main explanation and adds a short appendix with:
1) explicit Beta PDF formula (Gamma/Beta function form), and  
2) one complete numeric mixture-density computation end-to-end.

---

## 1) High-level algorithm

Radial-kernel-ips uses:

- a stochastic policy over trajectories in a 2D box
- kernel inverse-probability scaling of rewards
- group-normalized advantages
- PPO-style clipped ratio updates using old vs new trajectory likelihoods

The policy is “radial”:
- sample angle-latent \(u_\theta \in (0,1)\)
- sample radius-latent \(u_r \in (0,1)\)
- map to geometric action \(a=(a_x,a_y)\)

---

## 2) Policy outputs (\(K=2\))

Per state, policy emits \(1+6K=13\) numbers:

1. stop logit \(z_{\text{stop}}\)
2. theta branch:
   - mixture logits \(z^\theta_1,z^\theta_2\)
   - raw shapes \(\tilde\alpha^\theta_1,\tilde\alpha^\theta_2,\tilde\beta^\theta_1,\tilde\beta^\theta_2\)
3. radius branch:
   - mixture logits \(z^r_1,z^r_2\)
   - raw shapes \(\tilde\alpha^r_1,\tilde\alpha^r_2,\tilde\beta^r_1,\tilde\beta^r_2\)

Parameter conversion:

\[
P(\text{stop}\mid s)=\sigma(z_{\text{stop}}),\quad
P(\text{continue}\mid s)=1-\sigma(z_{\text{stop}})
\]

\[
w^\theta_k=\frac{e^{z^\theta_k}}{\sum_{j=1}^2 e^{z^\theta_j}},\quad
w^r_k=\frac{e^{z^r_k}}{\sum_{j=1}^2 e^{z^r_j}}
\]

\[
\alpha=\beta_{\min}+\beta_{\max}\sigma(\tilde\alpha),\quad
\beta=\beta_{\min}+\beta_{\max}\sigma(\tilde\beta)
\]

---

## 3) Stop decision details

Stop output is a **logit**, not probability directly.  
Probability is sigmoid-transformed.

Example:
- \(z_{\text{stop}}=0.7 \Rightarrow P(\text{stop})=\sigma(0.7)=0.668\)

Final terminal decision follows:
- sampled stop OR forced boundary-exit
- except at exact start state where stopping is disallowed

---

## 4) Theta/radius mixture interpretation

The latent densities are mixture-of-Beta:

\[
p_\theta(u_\theta\mid s)=\sum_{k=1}^{2} w^\theta_k\;\text{BetaPDF}(u_\theta;\alpha^\theta_k,\beta^\theta_k)
\]
\[
p_r(u_r\mid s)=\sum_{k=1}^{2} w^r_k\;\text{BetaPDF}(u_r;\alpha^r_k,\beta^r_k)
\]

This **is** a weighted sum in density space.

Important distinction:
- log_prob uses this mixture density
- sampling is: pick component from categorical, then draw from that component’s Beta

---

## 5) Meaning of \(\alpha,\beta\): “where mass lies on (0,1)”

\[
\text{BetaPDF}(u;\alpha,\beta)\propto u^{\alpha-1}(1-u)^{\beta-1},\quad u\in(0,1)
\]

Behavior:
- \(\alpha>\beta\): density toward 1
- \(\alpha<\beta\): density toward 0
- \((1,1)\): uniform
- both \(>1\): center-peaked
- both \(<1\): U-shaped

So \(\alpha,\beta\) shape where \(u\)-samples are likely.

---

## 6) From latent variables to action

Sample:
- stop/continue branch
- if continue, draw \(u_\theta,u_r\in(0,1)\)

Transform:
\[
\theta=\frac{\pi}{2}u_\theta,\quad
r=r_{\max}(s,\theta)\,u_r
\]
\[
a_x=r\cos\theta,\quad a_y=r\sin\theta
\]

where:
\[
r_{\max}(s,\theta)=\min(\delta,\text{boundary limit along direction }\theta)
\]

---

## 7) Why Jacobian factors appear in \(\pi(a\mid s)\)

Continuous density must be transformed under coordinate changes.

For non-terminal action:
\[
\pi(a\mid s)=P(\text{continue}\mid s)\cdot p_\theta(u_\theta\mid s)\cdot p_r(u_r\mid s)\cdot \frac{2}{\pi}\cdot\frac{1}{r_{\max}}\cdot\frac{1}{r}
\]

The three factors come from:
1. \(u_\theta\to\theta\): \(|du_\theta/d\theta|=2/\pi\)
2. \(u_r\to r\): \(|du_r/dr|=1/r_{\max}\)
3. polar\(\to\)Cartesian: \(|\partial(a_x,a_y)/\partial(\theta,r)|=r\Rightarrow\) inverse \(1/r\)

Without these factors, action likelihoods in Cartesian space are wrong and gradients become biased.

---

## 8) Log-likelihood forms

For continue action:
\[
\log\pi(a\mid s)=
\log P(\text{continue}\mid s)
+\log p_\theta(u_\theta\mid s)
+\log p_r(u_r\mid s)
-\log(\pi/2)-\log r_{\max}-\log r
\]

For terminal action:
\[
\log\pi(a_\dagger\mid s)=\log P(\text{stop}\mid s)
\]

---

## 9) Old vs new policy in PPO-style update

Trajectories are generated by old policy snapshot.

For each recorded \((s_t,a_t)\), evaluate:
- \(\log\pi_{\text{old}}(a_t|s_t)\) (stored)
- \(\log\pi_{\text{new}}(a_t|s_t)\) (recomputed during updates)

Trajectory sums:
\[
\log P_{\text{old}}=\sum_t \log\pi_{\text{old}}(a_t|s_t),\quad
\log P_{\text{new}}=\sum_t \log\pi_{\text{new}}(a_t|s_t)
\]
\[
r=\exp(\log P_{\text{new}}-\log P_{\text{old}})
\]

This ratio is clipped and multiplied by advantage.

---

## 10) Gradient flow to 13 outputs (\(K=2\))

For non-terminal action, gradients flow through:
- stop logit via \(\log P(\text{continue}|s)\)
- theta logits via mixture weights \(w^\theta\) in \(p_\theta\)
- theta raw shapes via \(\alpha^\theta,\beta^\theta\) inside BetaPDF
- radius logits via \(w^r\) in \(p_r\)
- radius raw shapes via \(\alpha^r,\beta^r\) inside BetaPDF

For terminal action:
- signal mainly through stop logit via \(\log P(\text{stop}|s)\)

Only \(\pi_{\text{new}}\) gets differentiated; \(\pi_{\text{old}}\) is fixed.

---

## 11) Numeric trajectory-level old/new example

Suppose trajectory has two decisions:
1. continue action \(a_1=(0.0394,0.0578)\)
2. terminal action

Given:
\[
\log\pi_{\text{old}}(a_1|s_1)=2.925,\quad \log\pi_{\text{old}}(a_2|s_2)=-0.288
\]
\[
\log P_{\text{old}}=2.637,\quad P_{\text{old}}\approx e^{2.637}=13.98
\]

\[
\log\pi_{\text{new}}(a_1|s_1)=2.412,\quad \log\pi_{\text{new}}(a_2|s_2)=-0.371
\]
\[
\log P_{\text{new}}=2.041,\quad P_{\text{new}}\approx e^{2.041}=7.70
\]

\[
r=\exp(2.041-2.637)=\exp(-0.596)\approx 0.551
\]

---

## 12) Reward and advantage context

After rollout:
1. compute terminal-state rewards
2. compute kernel density proxy \( \hat p_i \) within each group
3. scale reward: \( \tilde R_i = R_i/\hat p_i \)
4. group-normalize \( \tilde R_i \) to form advantages
5. optimize clipped-ratio objective + entropy regularization

---

## 13) Final summary

- Policy emits stop + mixture logits + Beta shape params for angle/radius
- Continuous action density is mixture-Beta density plus Jacobian correction
- Old/new trajectory likelihood ratio drives PPO update
- Backprop passes through stop path and both mixture-shape paths

# Appendix A — Explicit Beta PDF (Gamma/Beta function form)

For \(u\in(0,1)\), \(\alpha,\beta>0\):

\[
\text{BetaPDF}(u;\alpha,\beta)=\frac{u^{\alpha-1}(1-u)^{\beta-1}}{B(\alpha,\beta)}
\]

where Beta function:
\[
B(\alpha,\beta)=\int_0^1 t^{\alpha-1}(1-t)^{\beta-1}\,dt
\]

and in Gamma-function form:
\[
B(\alpha,\beta)=\frac{\Gamma(\alpha)\Gamma(\beta)}{\Gamma(\alpha+\beta)}
\]

So equivalent:
\[
\text{BetaPDF}(u;\alpha,\beta)
=
\frac{\Gamma(\alpha+\beta)}{\Gamma(\alpha)\Gamma(\beta)}
u^{\alpha-1}(1-u)^{\beta-1}
\]

For \(K=2\) mixture:
\[
p(u\mid s)=w_1\,\text{BetaPDF}(u;\alpha_1,\beta_1)+w_2\,\text{BetaPDF}(u;\alpha_2,\beta_2)
\]

# Appendix B — Full numeric mixture-density computation (end-to-end)

We compute one non-terminal action density \( \pi(a|s) \) completely.

Assume:
- \(s=(0.2,0.3)\), \(\delta=0.2\)
- action \(a=(0.0394,0.0578)\)
- so \(r=\sqrt{0.0394^2+0.0578^2}\approx 0.0700\)
- \(\theta=\operatorname{atan2}(0.0578,0.0394)\approx 0.973\)
- \(u_\theta=2\theta/\pi\approx 0.619\)
- \(r_{\max}=0.2\), so \(u_r=r/r_{\max}=0.35\)
- \(P(\text{continue}|s)=0.332\)

---

## B.1 Theta mixture density at \(u_\theta=0.619\)

Let:
- weights \(w^\theta=(0.818,0.182)\)
- component 1: \((\alpha,\beta)=(1.894,1.451)\)
- component 2: \((\alpha,\beta)=(0.907,2.004)\)

Using BetaPDF formula:
- \(\text{BetaPDF}_1(0.619)\approx 1.266\)
- \(\text{BetaPDF}_2(0.619)\approx 0.730\)

Then
\[
p_\theta(0.619)=0.818(1.266)+0.182(0.730)\approx 1.168
\]

---

## B.2 Radius mixture density at \(u_r=0.35\)

Let:
- weights \(w^r=(0.289,0.711)\)
- component 1: \((1.967,1.749)\)
- component 2: \((0.930,2.405)\)

Compute:
- \(\text{BetaPDF}_1(0.35)\approx 1.259\)
- \(\text{BetaPDF}_2(0.35)\approx 1.339\)

Then
\[
p_r(0.35)=0.289(1.259)+0.711(1.339)\approx 1.316
\]

---

## B.3 Jacobian factor

\[
J=\frac{2}{\pi}\cdot\frac{1}{r_{\max}}\cdot\frac{1}{r}
=
\frac{2}{\pi}\cdot\frac{1}{0.2}\cdot\frac{1}{0.07}
\approx 45.473
\]

---

## B.4 Final density

\[
\pi(a|s)=P(\text{continue}|s)\cdot p_\theta\cdot p_r\cdot J
\]
\[
\pi(a|s)=0.332\times 1.168\times 1.316\times 45.473
\approx 23.2
\]

This is a **density**, not a discrete probability, so it can be \(>1\).

Log form:
\[
\log\pi(a|s)\approx
\log 0.332+\log 1.168+\log 1.316+\log 45.473
\approx 3.14
\]

(Exact value differences vs earlier examples come from different illustrative intermediate numbers.)

---

# Appendix C — Which network outputs influence this numeric \(\pi(a|s)\)

In this example, non-terminal action likelihood depends on:

- stop logit \(z_{\text{stop}}\) via \(P(\text{continue}|s)\)
- theta logits \(z^\theta_1,z^\theta_2\) via \(w^\theta\)
- theta shape raws \(\tilde\alpha^\theta_k,\tilde\beta^\theta_k\) via BetaPDF values
- radius logits \(z^r_1,z^r_2\) via \(w^r\)
- radius shape raws \(\tilde\alpha^r_k,\tilde\beta^r_k\) via BetaPDF values

Hence all 12 continuous-branch outputs + stop logit can receive gradient for continue decisions.
Terminal decisions primarily train stop logit.
